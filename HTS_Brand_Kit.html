01 — The Symbol
The X-Star

The X-Star is composed of four fat diamond petals radiating from a central jewel. Each pair of opposing petals shares a color — seafoam on the NW/SE axis, navy on the NE/SW axis — creating a woven, textile-like visual rhythm. The coral center anchors the mark.

NW · SE Petals

Seafoam — #5AADA8
Evokes water, horizon, Hawaiian sea

NE · SW Petals

Navy — #2B5278
Depth, craft, trade authority

Center Jewel

Coral — #D4614A
The heart. Warmth, life, creative energy

Petal Construction

Each petal is built from two paths — a solid and a 50% opacity overlay — creating the layered, woven depth that references textile weaving

05 — Usage Guidelines
Do & Don't

✦ Do

  • Use the X-Star symbol exactly as defined — four petals, coral center
  • Use Cormorant Garamond Light (300) for all headings
  • Use Josefin Sans Thin/Light for all labels and UI text
  • Give the logo generous clear space — at minimum the width of one petal on all sides
  • Use Shell (#F8F4EC) as the default page background
  • Use the italic weight of Cormorant for warmth, feeling, and brand voice
  • Use Gold (#C8A96E) exclusively for fine rules, dividers, and premium accents
  • Pair Coral with Navy as the primary two-color system

✕ Don't

  • Don't rotate, stretch, or distort the X-Star symbol
  • Don't use the logo at less than 24px wide
  • Don't place the full-color logo on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Don't use bold weights of Josefin Sans — the brand is thin and spacious
  • Don't use purple, pink, or warm gradients — not in the palette
  • Don't stack multiple brand colors in a single layout without restraint
  • Don't use Coral as a background color for large areas — it should be an accent
  • Don't substitute other serif fonts for Cormorant Garamond
